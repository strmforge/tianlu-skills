# Conflict Resolution

Methods can overlap, conflict, or supersede each other.

## Default Rules

1. Narrow authority wins for high-risk actions.
2. Low-risk fast paths should remain available when no red-lane trigger exists.
3. More specific scope beats broad scope inside that domain.
4. Newer evidence can supersede older evidence only when source and eval quality are adequate.
5. Adapter constraints cannot weaken source principles silently.
6. Overlay preferences cannot represent themselves as runtime-neutral law.
7. Draft, checklist, and review should precede executable automation when risk is unclear.
8. When more than three laws seem relevant, choose a primary law and route companions through `index/dependency-map.md` instead of loading or restating every law.

Record conflicts in `index/conflict-map.md`.
