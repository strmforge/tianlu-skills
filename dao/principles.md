# Dao Principles

Dao contains source principles for the method system. It is not the full method collection.

## Principles

1. Neutrality is not flattening.
   - Do not erase diversity.
   - Put each method in its honest scope.

2. Scope before promotion.
   - A method must declare where it applies before it can be reused safely.
   - A narrow method can be valuable without being universal.

3. Evidence before activation.
   - External posts, repositories, docs, and tool outputs can suggest methods.
   - They do not authorize active behavior changes by themselves.

4. Adapters do not rewrite source principles.
   - Runtime-specific packages may translate methods into local formats.
   - They must not silently weaken source principles.

5. Overlays are scoped lineages.
   - Project, organization, machine, and personal methods belong in overlays when they are not general.
   - Overlay content must not pretend to be runtime-neutral law.

6. New methods enter through incubation.
   - Incubation is a path into the collection, not a rejection area.

7. Promotion requires validation.
   - A method can move upward only when evidence shows it works beyond its original scope.

8. Retirement is normal.
   - Methods can decay, be superseded, be marked unsafe, or be removed from active routing.
