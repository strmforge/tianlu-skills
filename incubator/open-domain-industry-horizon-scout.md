# Open-Domain Industry Horizon Scout

Status: incubating.

## Signal

A recurring feed-reading or X-reading workflow can become too passive if it only answers user-named topics or rotates through a fixed taxonomy. The user may later ask about an unfamiliar industry, or a new industry pattern may emerge before the user knows to ask. A useful scout should therefore maintain bounded, proactive coverage across industries, business models, operational surfaces, regulatory changes, tool shifts, open-source and community signals, messy-material organization methods, and failure cases.

## Proposed Trigger

Use this candidate when a workflow is asked to read broad external signals, watch X or community feeds, scan GitHub or open-source communities, follow current news or official releases, maintain an industry radar, build "360 industries" awareness, prepare for unknown future user questions, find messy-material organization methods, or self-evolve by discovering new domains before the user names them.

Use it especially when the target question is how a solo operator with one computer could earn money across any industry or subfield. That lens is allowed to guide scouting, but it does not narrow the scout to AI, software, creator work, X, or any single industry.

## Proposed Mechanism

1. Keep fixed categories as coverage scaffolding only.
2. Maintain a rotating industry map with at least these lanes:
   - production and supply chains;
   - commerce and marketplaces;
   - services and local operations;
   - regulated domains such as medical, finance, legal, education, housing, and travel;
   - creator, media, entertainment, and cultural production;
   - software, automation, agents, and data infrastructure;
   - emerging business models, new tools, and platform rule changes.
3. Treat logged-in feeds or communities as read-only source access unless separately authorized.
   - Reading through a logged-in browser session may be necessary for source access.
   - Do not treat logged-in access as permission to like, follow, bookmark, reply, post, DM, upload, download, export, scrape, change settings, join communities, buy, subscribe, or mutate accounts.
   - Record that a logged-in source was read only at the level needed for provenance; do not expose cookies, session state, profile internals, private identifiers, or unnecessary account details.
4. For each rotation, capture only bounded scout receipts:
   - source signal;
   - primary or official source to verify;
   - industry and value-chain location;
   - concrete user question it would help answer later;
   - process map;
   - solo-operator path, if any;
   - computer-deliverable artifact or service packet, if any;
   - buyer or user and first paid-proof candidate;
   - delivery workflow and required source access;
   - risk and authority boundary;
   - candidate type and existing Tianlu mapping;
   - next action: accept, reject, delay, watch, eval idea, playbook candidate, adapter candidate, or local checklist.
5. Promote only when a repeated mechanism, hard-negative, or reusable boundary appears.
6. Keep discoveries as receipts, incubator notes, eval ideas, or local checklists until evidence and scope justify promotion.

## Initial Scope

This candidate is about discovery and preparation, not execution. It may improve future research readiness, industry vocabulary, source maps, candidate routing, and solo-operator opportunity framing. It must not be used to claim professional advice, market timing, income certainty, business opportunity certainty, account-action authority, tool installation authority, external-send authority, publication authority, or durable memory authority.

## Known Failure Modes

- Staying inside a user-provided taxonomy and missing adjacent industries.
- Waiting for the user to name a field before building any source map.
- Treating X virality, engagement, founder posts, course claims, repo stars, or product launches as authority.
- Treating a one-person-one-computer opportunity hypothesis as proof of income before buyer evidence, deliverable shape, pricing hypothesis, and first paid proof exist.
- Treating logged-in X or community access as permission for account actions, state changes, scraping, downloads, exports, or private-session inspection.
- Overfitting one new industry example into broad Tianlu law.
- Collecting an oversized news archive instead of extracting mechanisms and boundaries.
- Using subagent fan-out or free model routes without route, cost, privacy, and source receipts.
- Turning discovery into installs, account mutations, outreach, publishing, spending, or regulated conclusions.

## Verification Needed

- Replay on several unrelated industries where the user did not preselect the field.
- Replay on several one-person-one-computer earning-path scans where the scout must identify buyer, deliverable, first proof, workflow, and stop boundary without claiming income certainty.
- Evidence that scout receipts improve later source-grounded answers without bloating the archive.
- Negative tests for passive taxonomy lock-in, trend-authority overreach, tool-install overreach, and account-action overreach.
- Review against `surface-memory-learning`, `surface-evidence-eval`, `surface-browser-runtime`, `surface-mcp-tools-auth`, and `research-to-brief-synthesis`.

## Placement Candidates

- Keep as incubator while the method is still being shaped.
- If stable, this may become a productive playbook for horizon scanning and source-map preparation.
- Eval coverage should remain narrow: proactive discovery is not authority to act, install, publish, contact, spend, or claim readiness.
