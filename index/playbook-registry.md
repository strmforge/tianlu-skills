# Playbook Registry

The playbook registry lists output-oriented methods and what each one helps an agent produce.

Playbooks are not permission grants. They are productive method cards. When a playbook touches account actions, money, publication, regulated claims, private data, or durable behavior, route to the supporting law family before acting.

| Playbook | Role | Primary Outputs | Scope | Supporting Laws |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| [Commerce Operations Planning](../playbooks/commerce-operations.md) | Helps agents plan and improve commerce work without binding to one marketplace. | Opportunity report, product page brief, title variants, promotion plan, customer-response drafts. | commerce-neutral | Browser/runtime, MCP/tools/auth, evidence/eval |
| [Long-Form Fiction Development](../playbooks/longform-fiction-development.md) | Helps agents develop fiction from premise through outline, continuity, drafting, and revision. | Premise, character sheets, world rules, conflict ladder, chapter outline, revision notes. | creative-writing-neutral | Memory/learning, evidence/eval |
| [Content Operations Planning](../playbooks/content-operations.md) | Helps agents operate topic, script, packaging, distribution, and feedback loops. | Topic pool, angle brief, script, format variants, publishing calendar draft, performance review. | content-operations-neutral | Browser/runtime, MCP/tools/auth, evidence/eval |

## Registry Rules

- Add productive methods here only after they have a method card in `playbooks/`.
- Name the useful output, not only the safety boundary.
- Keep platform-specific tricks, account-specific preferences, and project voice in `overlays/` or adapters.
- Use `laws/` only when a playbook crosses an authority, evidence, privacy, install, or durable-write boundary.
