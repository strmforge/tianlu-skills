# Playbook: Content Operations Planning

Status: seed draft

Scope: content-operations-neutral. This playbook supports content planning and operation across media formats. It is not tied to any single platform, account, niche, or creator persona.

## Purpose

Help an agent turn audience, topic, platform, script, distribution, and feedback signals into a repeatable content operating loop.

## Inputs

- Content goal: awareness, trust, education, conversion, community, retention, or research.
- Audience segment and known pain points.
- Topic sources: feed items, customer questions, search terms, comments, competitor posts, product roadmap, or personal notes.
- Platform constraints: format, length, media type, publishing cadence, claim policy, and interaction norms.
- Existing brand, voice, creator, or account positioning.
- Historical performance signals.

## Procedure

1. Build the topic pool.
   - Collect raw topic signals and label them by audience problem, urgency, novelty, proof availability, and fit to positioning.
   - Keep source links or source notes for factual claims.

2. Select an angle.
   - Convert a topic into a specific promise, point of view, audience, and desired reader or viewer action.
   - Produce multiple angles before choosing one.

3. Draft the content structure.
   - Choose the structure: story, tutorial, list, analysis, comparison, case breakdown, opinion, or update.
   - Produce the hook, core points, proof, transition, and close.

4. Produce format variants.
   - Adapt the same idea into post, thread, short video script, long video outline, newsletter, carousel, or landing-page section as needed.
   - Preserve the core promise while changing format mechanics.

5. Package the content.
   - Draft title, thumbnail or cover brief, caption, metadata, and call to action.
   - Mark any claim that needs proof or domain review.

6. Plan distribution.
   - Place content in a calendar by audience timing, platform cadence, campaign sequence, and production capacity.
   - Separate draft scheduling from live publication.

7. Review feedback.
   - Compare expected and actual reach, click, watch, completion, save, share, comment, conversion, and sentiment.
   - Feed the result into the next topic pool as scoped evidence, not universal law.

## Outputs

- Topic pool with source notes.
- Angle brief.
- Content outline or script.
- Platform-specific variants.
- Packaging brief.
- Publishing calendar draft.
- Performance review and next-cycle recommendation.

## Quality Signals

- The topic is tied to a real audience problem or desire.
- The angle is specific enough to reject weaker variants.
- The content has a visible structure and proof path.
- Platform variants preserve the same promise without being copied mechanically.
- Feedback produces a concrete next-cycle decision.

## Human Checkpoints

- Brand voice, persona, taste, and public stance.
- Publication, reply, DM, sponsorship, paid promotion, or account-setting actions.
- Legal, financial, medical, health, political, or regulated claims.
- Use of copyrighted material, user-generated content, private messages, or sensitive personal data.
