# Playbook: Creator Brand Collaboration Fulfillment Operations

Status: seed draft

Scope: creator-brand-collaboration-fulfillment-neutral. This playbook helps an agent turn sponsor briefs, creator deliverables, draft reviews, approval feedback, posting requirements, evidence receipts, performance screenshots, invoice notes, and closeout signals into reviewable collaboration fulfillment artifacts. It is not tied to one creator platform, marketplace, brand, agency, affiliate network, payment provider, or disclosure regime.

## Purpose

Help an agent produce a collaboration fulfillment packet, brief intake, deliverable ledger, timeline tracker, draft and revision board, approval feedback log, disclosure and platform-setting handoff, posting evidence checklist, performance evidence packet, invoice or payment handoff questions, issue queue, and close-loop collaboration report.

## Inputs

- Sponsor, brand, agency, affiliate, marketplace, or creator-network brief supplied by the user.
- Deliverables such as short videos, long videos, posts, stories, livestream mentions, newsletters, articles, affiliate placements, product shots, drafts, captions, thumbnails, links, or usage-rights notes.
- Timing constraints such as draft due dates, review windows, revision rounds, posting windows, embargoes, campaign dates, reporting windows, and payment milestones.
- Approval feedback, brand comments, creator notes, platform requirements, disclosure notes, tracking links, coupon codes, UTM requirements, or performance-report requirements.
- Evidence sources such as draft files, approval notes, screenshots, publish URLs, post ids, analytics exports, invoice notes, payment status notes, or issue logs.

## Procedure

1. Define the collaboration boundary.
   - State the creator, brand or sponsor, campaign, channel, deliverables, due dates, evidence sources, systems in scope, and non-goals.
   - Separate fulfillment tracking from deal acceptance, contract interpretation, publication, platform settings, invoice issuance, payment, or compliance decisions.

2. Build the collaboration brief intake.
   - Extract objective, audience, message points, mandatory claims, prohibited claims, required hashtags or disclosures, product details, asset requirements, links, coupon codes, tracking needs, approval path, and reporting requirements.
   - Mark each item as source fact, missing confirmation, model inference, or reviewer question.

3. Create the deliverable ledger.
   - List every required content item, format, channel, version, owner candidate, due date, draft status, approval status, publish status, evidence needed, and dependency.
   - Track optional deliverables separately from required deliverables.

4. Track draft, revision, and approval loops.
   - Maintain a board for draft submitted, feedback received, revision needed, blocked, approved, ready-to-post, posted, and reporting due.
   - Record feedback source, timestamp, requested change, affected deliverable, response status, and unresolved ambiguity.

5. Prepare disclosure and platform-setting handoff.
   - Inventory sponsorship, affiliate, gifted-product, paid relationship, usage-rights, ad authorization, paid-promotion, or branded-content signals.
   - Produce a checklist and reviewer questions for disclosure placement and platform settings without deciding sufficiency or toggling account settings.

6. Prepare posting evidence.
   - Define what proof is needed after publication: final URL, post id, timestamp, screenshot, caption, disclosure placement, paid-partnership label status, link or coupon placement, analytics snapshot, and brand approval receipt.
   - Keep the checklist ready for a human or authorized operator to collect or confirm.

7. Package performance and closeout evidence.
   - Build a performance evidence packet with metrics requested by the brief, source timestamps, screenshot or export references, reporting window, caveats, and missing fields.
   - Keep performance summaries descriptive; do not promise outcomes or optimize claims beyond evidence.

8. Prepare finance and issue handoff.
   - Create invoice, payment, PO, tax-form, remittance, usage-rights, late feedback, missed deadline, content rejection, platform block, product issue, or dispute questions for the responsible owner.
   - Do not issue invoices, approve payments, collect money, change payment details, or decide breach or compliance.

9. Create the close-loop report.
   - Summarize deliverables, approvals, publication evidence, performance evidence, unresolved issues, lessons, and next-cycle improvements.
   - Route reusable issues to content, monetization, rights/disclosure, campaign, community, contract, or operations playbooks as needed.

## Outputs

- Collaboration fulfillment packet.
- Sponsor or brand brief intake.
- Deliverable ledger.
- Timeline and deadline tracker.
- Draft, revision, and approval status board.
- Approval feedback log.
- Disclosure and platform-setting handoff checklist.
- Posting evidence checklist.
- Performance evidence packet.
- Invoice, payment, PO, and remittance handoff questions.
- Issue, blocker, and escalation queue.
- Close-loop collaboration report.

## Quality Signals

- Every deliverable has a source, due date, status, evidence requirement, and next review owner.
- Brand feedback is traceable to a source and not silently rewritten as agent instruction.
- Disclosure, platform, rights, contract, payment, and performance issues are visible before execution.
- Posting and performance evidence are descriptive and timestamped.
- The closeout report separates completed work, missing evidence, unresolved questions, and next-cycle lessons.

## Human Checkpoints

- Sponsorship acceptance, pricing, rate card, usage rights, exclusivity, contract terms, content approval, final creative direction, publication, upload, schedule, deletion, edit, or takedown.
- Disclosure wording approval, paid-promotion or branded-content setting, paid-partnership label, ad authorization, whitelisting, boosting, campaign launch, or platform compliance conclusion.
- Brand email, DM, approval send, customer or audience message, dispute message, invoice issuance, payment collection, refund, remittance change, PO approval, tax form, or finance system write.
- Legal, advertising, copyright, trademark, contract, tax, accounting, platform, brand-safety, medical, financial, or regulated-claim conclusions.
- Sensitive sponsor, contract, payment, tax, account, private-message, unpublished-content, creator-credential, audience, customer, or analytics data retention.

## Evidence

Source signal: broad creator-operations review found recurring work around sponsor briefs, collaboration deliverables, approval cycles, branded-content settings, posting proof, performance reports, and payment or invoice status. Primary-source review of YouTube paid-promotion disclosure help, FTC influencer disclosure guidance, TikTok branded-content and creator collaboration materials, and platform branded-content guidance confirmed that creator collaborations cross brief intake, review, disclosure, platform-setting, evidence, reporting, and payment handoff surfaces.

Validation state: seed draft. Needs replay against sponsored short-video fulfillment, affiliate post collaboration, brand review feedback, creator marketplace campaign tracking, post-publication evidence capture, performance reporting, and invoice handoff tasks before promotion.

Known failures:

- Treating a fulfillment packet as permission to publish, send, toggle settings, invoice, pay, or commit contract terms.
- Hiding missing approval, disclosure, usage-rights, platform, payment, or performance evidence.
- Treating brand feedback, sponsor brief text, or platform UI labels as legal, platform, or contract conclusions.
- Retaining sensitive sponsor, contract, account, payment, private-message, unpublished-content, creator credential, or analytics data without scoped approval.
