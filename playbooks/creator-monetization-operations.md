# Playbook: Creator Monetization Operations

Status: seed draft

Scope: creator-monetization-neutral. This playbook helps an agent turn audience, content, offer, sponsorship, affiliate, product, and revenue signals into reviewable creator monetization work products. It is not tied to one platform, creator persona, ad network, storefront, sponsor, or payment provider.

## Purpose

Help an agent produce a monetization map, audience-to-offer fit table, sponsor or affiliate opportunity ledger, disclosure and claim-risk checklist, rate-card draft, pitch packet, productization plan, experiment plan, and performance review.

## Inputs

- Audience segment, creator positioning, content themes, trust boundaries, and community expectations.
- Existing content, analytics, engagement, conversion, sales, subscriber, or sponsorship signals.
- Offer candidates such as sponsorships, affiliate links, memberships, digital products, services, courses, events, newsletters, commerce products, or licensing.
- Sponsor, brand, affiliate, marketplace, storefront, or platform constraints if provided by the user.
- Evidence for claims, commercial promises, audience fit, product fit, and disclosure needs.

## Procedure

1. Define the monetization boundary.
   - State the creator, audience, channel, offer family, time window, and non-goals.
   - Separate exploration from publication, signing, payment, ad use, or account changes.

2. Map audience and trust constraints.
   - Connect audience need, content promise, relationship context, and trust risk.
   - Mark offers that would conflict with positioning, audience expectations, or claim evidence.

3. Build the monetization option set.
   - Compare sponsorship, affiliate, membership, product, service, event, licensing, and commerce paths.
   - Record required assets, proof, operational load, disclosure needs, and review questions.

4. Draft commercial assets.
   - Produce a rate-card draft, sponsor fit note, pitch packet, media kit outline, affiliate content brief, productization brief, or launch content plan as needed.
   - Keep claims and performance numbers tied to sources.

5. Review disclosures and claim risk.
   - Mark material connection, sponsorship, affiliate, gifted product, testimonial, typical-results, health, financial, legal, or safety claim risks for review.
   - Do not make a legal compliance determination.

6. Design the experiment loop.
   - Define testable offers, channels, metrics, stop criteria, audience feedback signals, and next-cycle decisions.
   - Treat revenue projections as assumptions, not promises.

## Outputs

- Creator monetization map.
- Audience-to-offer fit table.
- Sponsor, affiliate, or partner opportunity ledger.
- Disclosure and claim-risk checklist.
- Rate-card draft.
- Pitch packet or media kit outline.
- Productization or offer brief.
- Monetization experiment plan.
- Performance review and next-cycle recommendation.

## Quality Signals

- Each monetization option is tied to audience evidence, content fit, and operational capacity.
- Sponsorship, affiliate, testimonial, and claim risks are visible before publication.
- Revenue assumptions are separated from verified performance.
- The plan protects audience trust instead of optimizing only short-term income.

## Human Checkpoints

- Sponsorship acceptance, contract terms, exclusivity, pricing, revenue share, affiliate participation, product launch, refund policy, or customer promise.
- Publication, ad spend, paid promotion, DM, email send, platform account action, storefront action, or payment setup.
- Legal, financial, medical, health, safety, employment, or regulated claims.
- Disclosure wording, compliance decision, tax treatment, and final commercial commitment.

## Evidence

Source signal: creator monetization, endorsement disclosure, affiliate marketing, sponsorship review, and user correction that Tianlu must help real creator and media-manager agents produce work rather than only adding safety gates.

Validation state: seed draft. Needs replay on real creator offer planning, sponsorship review, affiliate content review, media kit drafting, and monetization experiment tasks before promotion.

Known failures:

- Treating income projections as guaranteed revenue.
- Hiding sponsorship, affiliate, gifted, or material-connection risks.
- Optimizing monetization while damaging audience trust.
- Publishing, signing, sending, or changing account or payment state without explicit approval.
