# Playbook: Creator Rights And Disclosure Clearance Planning

Status: seed draft

Scope: creator-rights-disclosure-clearance-neutral. This playbook helps an agent turn creator content, source assets, music, images, clips, transcripts, quotes, user-generated content, brand partnerships, sponsorships, affiliate relationships, paid promotion, platform reuse, copyright claims, Content ID matches, disclosure rules, and pre-publication review signals into reviewable rights and disclosure clearance artifacts. It is not tied to one platform, creator, brand, jurisdiction, music library, stock-asset service, legal system, disclosure regime, monetization program, or publication channel.

## Purpose

Help an agent produce useful pre-publication work products: source-asset inventory, rights and license ledger, missing-permission question list, reuse and transformation note, platform-policy check packet, sponsorship and affiliate disclosure map, disclosure wording draft packet, claim-evidence table, publication-risk question list, escalation queue, and review handoff packet.

The playbook supports preparation and review. It does not decide legality, copyright ownership, fair use, trademark clearance, advertising compliance, contract compliance, platform compliance, or publication approval, and it does not publish, upload, submit disputes, send takedowns, file appeals, buy licenses, contact rights holders, or change account settings.

## Inputs

- Draft post, script, video outline, short-form clip, long-form video, article, newsletter, podcast, live-stream plan, carousel, thumbnail, caption, product placement, or publishing plan supplied by the user.
- Source assets such as music, sound effects, images, video clips, screenshots, memes, quotes, transcripts, datasets, user comments, creator clips, brand materials, logos, product photos, stock assets, or platform-native sounds.
- Source metadata: asset owner or source, URL, license text, purchase receipt, platform library status, brand-provided terms, creator permission, date captured, intended channel, territory, monetization status, and planned transformation.
- Brand, sponsor, affiliate, paid partnership, gifted product, employee, agency, referral, creator network, or revenue-share relationship supplied by the user.
- Platform, community, monetization, copyright, branded-content, commercial-music, disclosure, ad, and reuse policy snippets supplied by the user.
- System surfaces described at a high level, such as social platforms, video platforms, music libraries, stock-asset services, CMS, ad accounts, creator dashboards, review tools, document stores, spreadsheets, or legal intake systems.

## Procedure

1. Define the clearance boundary.
   - Name the content item, publication channel, audience, monetization state, source-asset classes, brand or affiliate relationships, platform policy sources, reviewer owner, systems in scope, and non-goals.
   - Separate review artifacts from live publication, upload, license purchase, platform appeal, takedown, rights-holder contact, ad activation, and account actions.

2. Build the source-asset inventory.
   - List every music, image, clip, text excerpt, quote, logo, brand asset, user-generated item, third-party post, screenshot, dataset, or platform-native sound used in the draft.
   - Record source, link or identifier, capture date, supplied permission, license text, commercial-use status if supplied, planned use, and missing fields.

3. Map rights, reuse, and transformation questions.
   - For each asset, create a rights ledger with supplied license, permitted use claim, transformation note, attribution need, territory or channel limitation, monetization limitation, expiration, revocation risk, and evidence gap.
   - Keep fair-use, copyright, trademark, likeness, publicity-rights, moral-rights, and platform-policy questions as review questions, not conclusions.

4. Prepare sponsorship and disclosure review.
   - Identify paid, gifted, affiliate, referral, employment, agency, partnership, brand, creator network, or other material relationship signals.
   - Draft clear disclosure wording options and placement notes for human review.
   - Separate disclosure drafting from legal sufficiency, platform setting changes, ad labels, paid-partnership toggles, and publication approval.

5. Check platform and monetization surfaces.
   - Build a platform-check packet for reused content, repetitive content, commercial music, copyrighted material, branded content, paid promotion, ad policy, community standards, monetization eligibility, and claim or strike risk.
   - Record policy source, version or access date when available, affected channel, uncertainty, and reviewer questions.
   - Do not certify platform compliance or account safety.

6. Prepare revision and escalation options.
   - Convert gaps into options such as replace asset, remove asset, request permission, add attribution, change music, remove monetization, revise disclosure, alter thumbnail, re-record segment, or route to legal, brand, platform, or rights owner review.
   - Mark blocked items that require official source review, license verification, contract review, or human approval.

7. Draft the clearance handoff packet.
   - Produce an unsent reviewer packet with asset ledger, disclosure drafts, missing evidence, platform questions, escalation queue, and proposed safe next review actions.
   - Keep all customer, sponsor, platform, rights-holder, legal, or public messages unsent unless separately approved.

8. Create the learning loop.
   - Convert recurring clearance issues into source-library rules, creator brief updates, brand-intake questions, reusable disclosure snippets, asset intake checklists, or content operations backlog items.
   - Route production, repurposing, monetization, claim, or feedback issues to the appropriate companion playbook.

## Outputs

- Creator rights and disclosure clearance brief.
- Source-asset inventory.
- Rights and license ledger.
- Missing-permission and missing-evidence question list.
- Reuse, transformation, attribution, and monetization-limitation note.
- Sponsorship, affiliate, gifted-product, brand, or paid-relationship disclosure map.
- Disclosure wording draft packet.
- Platform policy and monetization check packet.
- Copyright claim, Content ID, reuse, commercial-music, branded-content, or ad-policy review queue.
- Publication-risk question list.
- Revision and escalation option table.
- Legal, brand, platform, rights-holder, or reviewer handoff packet.
- Post-publication evidence capture checklist.
- Creator clearance learning backlog.

## Quality Signals

- The output helps a creator or operator see what must be reviewed before publishing.
- Each asset, relationship, disclosure, or policy concern is tied to supplied evidence or marked as missing.
- Draft disclosures are clear, prominent, and reviewable without claiming legal sufficiency.
- The ledger separates source facts, license text, user assumptions, model inferences, and reviewer questions.
- The plan offers practical revision options instead of only saying "do not publish."
- The handoff identifies which issues need official-source, platform, brand, legal, rights-holder, or domain-owner review.
- The plan includes post-publication evidence to capture: final asset list, final disclosure text, platform settings, publish URL, claim id, dispute id, takedown id, license receipt, reviewer approval, or policy source date.

## Evidence

Source signal: broad X and domain scout review found recurring creator risks around reused content, copyright claims, Content ID matches, commercial music, asset repurposing, brand partnerships, affiliate or sponsored content, disclosure wording, and platform monetization eligibility. Primary-source review of YouTube reused-content and copyright-help materials, TikTok branded-content and commercial-music guidance, FTC social-media influencer disclosure guidance, and U.S. Copyright Office fair-use material confirmed that creator publication crosses rights, licensing, disclosure, monetization, platform-policy, and legal-review boundaries.

Validation state: seed draft. Needs replay against short-form video drafts, long-form video scripts, brand partnership posts, affiliate content, music-backed clips, repurposed webinar clips, quote-heavy newsletters, thumbnail reviews, copyright claim packets, and sponsored-content review packets before promotion.

Known failures:

- Treating a clearance packet as permission to publish, upload, monetize, buy licenses, submit disputes, file appeals, contact rights holders, or change platform settings.
- Treating disclosure wording, attribution, "educational use," "credit given," or "transformed enough" as legal sufficiency.
- Hiding missing source, license, permission, brand, or platform-policy evidence behind a green-light recommendation.
- Claiming fair use, copyright ownership, trademark clearance, advertising compliance, or platform compliance without scoped professional or domain-owner review.
- Retaining sensitive creator, brand, account, contract, payment, license, rights-holder, private-message, child, or unpublished-content data without scoped approval.
