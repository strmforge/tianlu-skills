# Playbook: Solo Business Lifecycle Planning

Status: seed draft

Scope: solo-business-lifecycle-neutral. This playbook helps an agent turn early business signals into a staged lifecycle packet for a solo operator, small venture, creator business, service offer, commerce idea, or digital product. It is not tied to one platform, industry, company size, market, payment provider, or growth model.

## Purpose

Help an agent produce an end-to-end early business plan that moves from community discovery to demand validation, manual MVP, first customers, pricing, marketing readiness, sustainable growth, operating values, and minimalist review.

This playbook is a coordinator over narrower productive playbooks. Use it when the user needs the full path, not only a listing plan, content calendar, sales packet, pricing note, or experiment plan.

## Inputs

- Business idea, offer candidate, service concept, product candidate, content niche, or audience hypothesis.
- Community, audience, market, search, review, comment, sales, support, or competitor signals.
- Observed pains, jobs to be done, buying context, willingness-to-pay signals, objections, and language from the target audience.
- Operator constraints: time, budget, skills, tools, inventory, fulfillment capacity, risk tolerance, legal or regulated boundaries, and preferred channels.
- Existing assets: content, customer list, storefront, prototype, community access, domain expertise, supplier notes, service process, or prior experiments.
- Current stage: discovery, validation, manual delivery, first customer, first revenue, repeatable offer, channel testing, growth, or review.
- Success metric, stop criteria, and human decisions that must not be automated.

## Procedure

1. Define the stage and boundary.
   - State the current lifecycle stage, target outcome, time window, capacity limit, and non-goals.
   - Separate planning artifacts from external actions such as outreach, publication, payment setup, price change, ad spend, account writes, or customer commitments.

2. Map the community before choosing the build.
   - Identify who has the problem, where they gather, what language they use, what triggers urgency, and what alternatives they already use.
   - Produce a community map with source signals, pain clusters, buying context, trust constraints, and unanswered questions.

3. Validate demand with evidence, not praise.
   - Separate compliments, likes, follows, wishful comments, waitlist clicks, interview claims, repeat pain, budget authority, deposit, pre-order, paid pilot, referral, and renewal signals.
   - Produce a demand evidence ledger and validation gap list.

4. Shape a manual MVP.
   - Prefer concierge, service, spreadsheet, draft packet, manual fulfillment, or small-scope workflow delivery before building a full product.
   - Define the smallest useful deliverable, intake requirements, delivery steps, quality bar, failure modes, and refund or stop questions for human review.

5. Plan first-customer acquisition.
   - Create a deep-acquisition plan for interviews, pilots, warm outreach, community participation, referrals, or direct sales.
   - Draft outreach and conversation packets, but do not send messages, scrape contacts, mutate CRM, or bypass platform rules.

6. Draft pricing and early revenue assumptions.
   - Compare value, customer segment, delivery cost, margin, risk, support load, and alternative prices.
   - Produce pricing hypotheses, payment questions, and first-revenue checkpoints without setting live prices, charging customers, issuing invoices, or promising revenue.

7. Gate marketing until proof exists.
   - Decide whether the business is ready for content, campaigns, ads, partnerships, marketplace listings, SEO, events, or creator channels.
   - If proof is weak, produce a learning plan instead of a marketing plan.

8. Plan sustainable growth.
   - Review capacity, gross margin, repeatability, retention, delivery quality, support burden, inventory or supplier risk, founder energy, and trust risk.
   - Produce growth options with stop conditions, not a scale-at-all-costs plan.

9. Define operating values.
   - Capture decision principles for the solo operator: customer promise, no-go lines, quality bar, communication standard, refund posture, evidence standard, and tradeoff rules.
   - Keep values operational, short, and testable.

10. Run the minimalist review loop.
    - Summarize what was observed, what was sold or not sold, what was delivered, what failed, what changed, and what the next small move is.
    - Convert feedback into backlog, experiment, content, sales, operations, or finance follow-up packets.

## Outputs

- Lifecycle stage map.
- Community and audience map.
- Demand evidence ledger.
- Manual MVP delivery plan.
- First-customer acquisition and conversation packet.
- Pricing hypothesis and first-revenue checkpoint sheet.
- Marketing readiness note or learning plan.
- Sustainable growth option map.
- Operating values card.
- Minimalist review sheet and next-cycle action queue.

## Quality Signals

- The plan starts with a community or buyer context, not only a product idea.
- Willingness-to-pay evidence is separated from praise, interest, vanity metrics, and AI inference.
- The MVP is manual, small, reviewable, and deliverable by the current operator.
- Marketing is gated by proof of problem, offer, delivery, and trust fit.
- Pricing considers value, cost, margin, risk, support load, and customer segment.
- Growth protects capacity, reliability, customer trust, and operator sustainability.
- The review loop produces a next action, not vague motivation.

## Human Checkpoints

- Final offer selection, pricing, discount, refund posture, payment setup, invoice issuance, contract terms, or revenue commitment.
- Outreach send, DM, email, community post, public reply, publication, ad spend, campaign launch, partnership acceptance, or marketplace listing.
- Account actions, CRM writes, seller dashboard writes, payment provider actions, analytics writes, platform settings, or customer data retention.
- Legal, medical, financial, tax, employment, insurance, safety, product-compliance, advertising-compliance, or regulated claims.
- Use of private customer lists, scraped contacts, logged-in communities, sensitive business data, or personal data.

## Evidence

Source signal: public social post framing a one-person business path as nine agent skills: find community, validate demand, manual MVP, first customers, pricing, marketing, sustainable growth, values, and minimalist review.

Validation state: seed draft. Needs replay on real solo-operator, creator, commerce, service, digital-product, and local-business planning tasks before promotion.

Known failures:

- Treating a motivational business sequence as proof that a specific offer will work.
- Starting with product build or marketing before community and paid-demand evidence.
- Turning a lifecycle packet into permission to send outreach, collect payment, publish, run ads, change prices, or mutate accounts.
- Optimizing growth while ignoring delivery capacity, margin, customer trust, or operator sustainability.
